## What a Speech Bubble Meme Actually Is

Most meme formats use built-in captions (top text / bottom text, like Drake or Distracted Boyfriend). A speech bubble meme is different — you're adding a **floating text bubble** onto any image, turning a normal photo into a "quote" from whoever's in it.

That's why it's so common on Twitter/X: screenshot someone's profile picture or a reaction photo, slap a speech bubble over it, and you've made them "say" the joke.

## Tips for Making It Actually Funny

**Match the bubble to the face.** A speech bubble lands hardest when there's a gap between what the bubble says and what the person's expression suggests. Confused face + confident statement is a reliable combo.

**Keep the text short.** Speech bubbles have limited space and the format reads best as a single short line — think tweet-length, not paragraph.

**Use real screenshots when you can.** Speech bubble memes about specific tweets, group chats, or screenshots hit harder than generic stock photos because the audience recognizes the context.
